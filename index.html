Where Longevity Meets the Brain: Aging Suppressors and Sex Chromosomes in the Mouse Brain Atlas

Cathy Liu · cathyliu014@gmail.com · personal exploratory project, July 2026
klotho aging suppressors X chromosome sex chromosomes Allen Brain Atlas Python

The Dubal lab investigates the biology of longevity from two complementary angles: the role of aging-suppressor proteins like klotho and FGF21 in protecting the brain, and the role of sex chromosome dosage in determining why females tend to live longer and are more resilient to certain brain diseases. I was curious how these two threads intersect at the transcriptional level — specifically, where in the brain are the aging suppressors expressed, and how broadly are X-chromosome escapee genes (which are present at double dosage in XX individuals) distributed across brain structures?

To explore this, I pulled in situ hybridization expression data from the Allen Mouse Brain Atlas for eight aging suppressor / longevity genes and four X-chromosome escapee genes across eight brain regions spanning cortex, hippocampus, cerebellum, and subcortical structures.

What this is. A personal exploratory analysis, not novel research. All data from the Allen Mouse Brain Atlas public API (Allen Institute for Brain Science, 2004). ISH expression energy is a bulk regional metric (sum of pixel intensity × density, normalized by structure volume). Analysis and interpretation are mine alone.

Part 1 — Aging Suppressor Genes

Klotho (Kl) peaks in cerebellum (3.61) and hippocampus (2.97) — consistent with the known enrichment of klotho in cerebellar Purkinje cells and hippocampal neurons. Isocortex (1.32) and thalamus (1.83) show moderate expression. Fgf21 (0.64) and Igf1 (1.01) both peak in olfactory bulb, while their hippocampal expression (0.11 and 0.25 respectively) is lower — suggesting that for FGF21 and IGF1, peripheral or distant brain sources supply the hippocampus rather than local production.

Several patterns are biologically interpretable:

  • Klotho's cerebellar and hippocampal concentration is consistent with the Dubal lab's finding that klotho elevation (via peripheral or CSF delivery) enhances hippocampal synaptic plasticity. Local hippocampal klotho expression suggests these neurons are both producers and targets of the longevity signal — a short-range autocrine or paracrine loop that could be disrupted in aging or Alzheimer's disease.
  • Gdf11 peaks in thalamus (1.58) and olfactory bulb (1.57), Gdf15 in olfactory bulb (0.33), both relatively low in hippocampus. If "young blood" factors like GDF11 act on the brain, these expression patterns suggest they do so via circuits beyond the canonical hippocampal plasticity targets — or that they act primarily on the brain via delivery through CSF from distant sources.
  • Tert (telomerase reverse transcriptase) is expressed in isocortex (1.37) and hippocampus (1.24) at the highest levels among the aging suppressors in cortical regions, consistent with neural progenitor activity and telomere maintenance in aging neurons.

Part 2 — X-Chromosome Escapee Genes

Several X-escapee genes show robust expression throughout the brain:

  • Kdm5c (KDM5C / JARID1C, H3K4me3 demethylase) is the most broadly and highly expressed of the group — expression energy 6.98 in isocortex, 6.85 in olfactory bulb, 4.65 in hippocampus. This chromatin regulator shapes the active promoter landscape genome-wide. In XX individuals, double dosage of Kdm5c could systematically maintain a more youthful chromatin state across brain cell types.
  • Usp9x (ubiquitin protease, X-linked, escapee) is broadly expressed across all regions (2.3–6.0 expression energy) and is required for synaptic protein stability. Dosage differences in Usp9x between XX and XY brains could influence the rate of protein aggregation and clearance in aging neurons.
  • Kdm6a (UTX, H3K27me3 demethylase) peaks in olfactory bulb (4.54) and cerebellum (2.93), with strong hippocampal expression (2.10). The convergence of Kdm6a and klotho in cerebellum and hippocampus is intriguing: UTX-mediated H3K27 demethylation could regulate klotho transcription itself in these same neurons, linking the sex chromosome dosage and longevity-factor axes at the chromatin level.

Why this connects to the lab's open questions

  • "How do sex chromosomes impact longevity and disease susceptibility?" — The X-escapee gene expression map provides a starting point for asking which brain structures are most influenced by sex chromosome dosage. KDM6A in choroid plexus, and KDM5C broadly across cortex and hippocampus, are positioned to exert systematic effects on chromatin state and gene regulation that could differ between XX and XY brains.
  • "How do aging-suppressors impact the brain?" — The cerebellar and hippocampal concentration of Kl, and the hippocampal expression of Tert, place these factors at the intersection of the brain's most aging-vulnerable circuits. Understanding what regulates their expression in hippocampal neurons — and how that changes with aging or disease — seems like a high-leverage question, especially in light of the lab's finding that CSF or peripheral klotho elevation rescues cognitive decline.
  • "Can anti-aging strategies prevent cognitive decline?" — The hippocampal co-expression of klotho and the X-escapee chromatin regulators Kdm6a and Kdm5c suggests that longevity-factor production and chromatin state maintenance converge on the hippocampus. These two axes — aging suppressor expression and sex chromosome dosage effects on chromatin — could potentially be targeted in combination to extend cognitive healthspan.

Methods

Data retrieved July 2026. Allen Mouse Brain Atlas (Allen Institute for Brain Science, 2004), in situ hybridization, adult mouse (P56), via RMA API at api.brain-map.org/api/v2/data/query.json. Structures: Midbrain (id 313), Isocortex (315), Hippocampal formation (1089), Cerebellum (512), Hypothalamus (1097), Striatum/Caudoputamen (672), Olfactory bulb (507), Thalamus (549). Expression metric: expression_energy from StructureUnionize (first ISH dataset returned per gene). Ddx3x excluded (no expression data in this dataset). Figures generated with pure Python (SVG).
